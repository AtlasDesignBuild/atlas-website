Local knowledge

Finishing basements in Newtown Borough and Newtown Township

Newtown Borough homes present the most technically demanding basement conditions in the community. Properties built before 1900 along State Street and the surrounding blocks frequently have stone foundations, dirt or original concrete slab floors and ceiling heights that leave little margin after mechanical runs are accounted for. We have encountered original fieldstone construction and early block foundations in this area that require moisture remediation before a single stud wall goes up. Finishing over untreated moisture is the most common reason basements fail within a few years of completion.

Newtown Borough is also an EPA Lead Safe regulated environment for any home built before 1978, which covers a substantial portion of the borough's housing stock. We are certified and handle all required protocols as a standard part of the project scope, not an add-on.

Newtown Township is a different environment. Homes built from the 1970s through the 2000s across the township generally have poured concrete foundations, drainage systems and ceiling heights that start at seven and a half to eight feet before any ductwork is factored in. These basements are more straightforward to finish from a structural standpoint, though moisture is still a variable throughout Bucks County's clay-heavy soils and we address it before framing regardless of when the home was built.

Newtown Borough and Newtown Township are separate municipalities with separate permit offices. Permit timelines can differ between the two. We handle both jurisdictions and factor the appropriate timeline into every project schedule from the start.

Our scope

What our basement finishing services include in Newtown

Every basement we finish starts with an assessment of the existing conditions. From there we handle design, permitting, construction and finish selection, start to finish.

Moisture assessment and waterproofing

We assess existing waterproofing, drainage and humidity before any framing begins. Moisture management is the foundation of a basement that lasts.

Framing, insulation and drywall

Code-compliant framing with proper insulation and moisture barriers, finished with drywall and trim that matches the quality of the rest of the home.

Bathroom and plumbing additions

Full bathrooms, powder rooms and wet bars including rough-in plumbing through the slab handled by licensed plumbers we work with on every project.

Custom built-ins and millwork

Media walls, built-in bookcases, bar cabinetry and custom storage designed for the space and built to last.

Flooring

Engineered hardwood, luxury vinyl plank, tile and carpet selected to work with the moisture profile of the space and the overall design direction.

Lighting design and electrical

Recessed lighting, accent lighting and dedicated circuits planned as part of the overall design rather than added as an afterthought.

Egress windows and code compliance

Code-compliant egress windows required for sleeping rooms, stairway clearances and ceiling heights all factored in from the start.

Permitting and inspections, handled by us

We pull permits, coordinate inspections and keep the project on schedule throughout.
