How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Bucks County, PA?

By Atlas Design & Build · Kitchen remodeling

Most kitchen remodels in Bucks County run from the mid five figures for a focused update into the six figures for a full custom renovation with new cabinetry and a reworked layout. Where your project lands depends on scope, materials, structural changes and the condition of the existing space.

A kitchen is the most used room in the house and the most complex to renovate. Cabinetry, countertops, appliances, plumbing, electrical and lighting all converge in one space, and the final cost reflects how much of that you change. The guide below explains the ranges, what moves them and how to plan a budget that holds from the first conversation through the final walkthrough.

What drives a kitchen remodel up or down

Two kitchens of the same size can land at very different numbers. These are the factors that move the cost the most:

  • Layout changes. Keeping plumbing and gas where they are holds costs down. Moving a sink, range or island adds plumbing, electrical and sometimes structural work.
  • Cabinetry. Stock cabinetry sits at the entry point, semi custom in the middle and full custom cabinetry built for your space at the top.
  • Countertops and materials. Quartz, natural stone and wood each carry different costs, and the same is true for tile, flooring and hardware.
  • Appliances. Integrated and professional grade appliances raise both the equipment cost and the installation scope.
  • Structural and systems work. Removing a wall, upgrading electrical service or rerouting plumbing all add to the figure.
  • The condition of the existing space. Older homes can hide surprises behind the walls. We plan for that upfront so it does not become a surprise later.

Custom metal and glass work is one of our specialties. Details like a custom range hood surround or a glass partition are handled in house rather than subcontracted, which keeps the quality and the schedule under one roof.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

Most kitchen renovations run six to twelve weeks of active construction once design and permits are complete. The full timeline from first conversation to final walkthrough generally lands between three and five months. We build the schedule with you upfront and share it in writing so you always know what is happening and what is next.

How to budget with confidence

The cleanest way to avoid surprises is to define the project in detail before any work begins. After the first conversation you receive a written proposal outlining the full scope of work, materials, timeline and pricing. We refine it together so you have a clear picture of what is included and what is not. That document, not a rough verbal estimate, is what protects your budget.

The takeaway

A kitchen remodel in Bucks County is a meaningful investment, and the range is wide because the decisions are yours to make. Scope, materials and layout changes are the levers. The right partner helps you understand those tradeoffs early, prices the work honestly and plans it carefully so the finished kitchen is worth what you put into it.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a small kitchen remodel cost in Bucks County?

Smaller kitchens and focused updates that keep the existing layout generally start in the mid five figures. The savings come from leaving plumbing and electrical in place and choosing stock or semi custom cabinetry rather than full custom.

Does a kitchen remodel add value to a home?

A well planned kitchen renovation is one of the most reliable improvements you can make to how a home looks, functions and feels. The lasting value comes from quality work and a layout that fits how you live, not from chasing trends that age quickly.

Do you handle the design as well as the construction?

Yes. As a design build firm, design and construction are handled under one roof. You work with a single team from layout planning through final installation, which keeps decisions consistent and avoids the gaps that appear between separate design and construction firms.
